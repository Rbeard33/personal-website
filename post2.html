Origins & History

Friday the 13th has long been associated with bad luck, but its origins go back to ancient times. Many believe it stems from Norse mythology, where Loki, the trickster god, crashed a dinner of 12 gods, making him the unlucky 13th guest. In Christianity, the superstition is linked to the Last Supper, where Judas was the 13th attendee. Combine this with the belief that Friday was the day of Jesus's crucifixion, and you have a recipe for superstition.
