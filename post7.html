How to Survive on Your Own After a Nuclear Event in Wisconsin

Introduction

The idea of a nuclear event hitting the United States is terrifying, but with the right preparation, it’s possible to survive the aftermath. In this guide, we’ll focus on Wisconsin, a state with cold winters, abundant natural resources, and plenty of rural areas that can aid survival. This plan assumes you’ve had a month to prepare and will need to last on your own for one month after the event.

1. Prepare Your Shelter: Creating a Safe Zone

If you’re within or near an impact zone, sheltering immediately is essential. Since Wisconsin has a mix of urban and rural areas, the ideal scenario is to shelter in a basement or cellar for maximum protection from radiation.

  • Reinforce Your Basement or Cellar: Stock it with thick materials (concrete, sandbags, or even stacks of books) along walls and doors to help block radiation.
  • Ventilation & Filtration: A hand-crank ventilation system with filters (HEPA or activated carbon) can help limit radiation exposure if you need fresh air.
  • Basic Furnishings and Comfort: Add blankets, sleeping bags, and foam mats to stay warm and comfortable during cold nights.

2. Stockpile Supplies: Essential Items to Gather Beforehand

With a month to prepare, you’ll want to focus on stocking up on critical resources for survival.

  • Food and Water: Stock up on non-perishable foods like canned goods, dried fruit, nuts, rice, beans, and powdered milk. Aim for 15 gallons of water per person, with a filtration system for more.
  • Medical Supplies: Have a first aid kit, antibiotics, pain relievers, potassium iodide tablets, and prescription medications.
  • Protection Against Radiation: Radiation suits, or DIY solutions with ponchos and plastic sheeting, and a Geiger counter to monitor radiation levels.

3. Radiation Awareness: Reducing Exposure

Minimizing exposure is critical.

  • Stay Inside for 48 Hours: Fallout radiation is highest within the first two days. Staying in a sealed space during this period significantly reduces exposure.
  • Limit Outdoor Activity: If you must go outside, wear goggles, masks, gloves, and layers.
  • Decontaminate When Returning Inside: Remove outer layers and wash thoroughly to remove any radioactive dust.

4. Heating and Power: Staying Warm in Wisconsin’s Cold Climate

Surviving Wisconsin’s cold is challenging without heating. Use propane or kerosene heaters, thermal blankets, and layer clothing.

5. Mental Preparation: Staying Focused and Alert

Maintaining mental resilience is key.

  • Develop a Routine: Create a schedule that includes basic tasks and hobbies to stay occupied.
  • Limit News Consumption: Listen to only essential updates to avoid overwhelming anxiety.
  • Stay Connected When Possible: If you have family or a survival group, coordinate and check in when safe.

Conclusion

Surviving a nuclear event is no small feat, but with one month to prepare, it’s possible to increase your odds. In Wisconsin, understanding your surroundings, preparing a secure shelter, and leveraging natural resources are essential steps. Focus on the basics: secure shelter, safe food and water, and mental resilience. With a plan and determination, you can make it through the toughest conditions.
