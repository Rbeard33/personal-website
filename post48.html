How to Prevent and Treat Colds Using Dr. Huberman's Protocol

When cold and flu season hits, being prepared is key. This guide inspired by Dr. Andrew Huberman offers actionable steps to prevent colds and treat symptoms effectively using science-backed strategies.

Prevention: Building a Resilient Immune System

  • Vitamin D3: 2,000–5,000 IU daily. Supports immune health. Recommended: Nature Made Vitamin D3.
  • Vitamin C: 500–1,000 mg daily. Supports immune resilience. Recommended: NOW Foods Vitamin C Powder.
  • Zinc: 15–30 mg daily. Boosts immune function. Recommended: Thorne Zinc Picolinate.
  • Probiotics: Promotes gut health for better immunity. Recommended: Garden of Life Probiotic.
  • Saline Nasal Rinse: Reduces viral load in nasal passages. Recommended: NeilMed Sinus Rinse.

Cold Treatment: What to Do When You’re Sick

  • Zinc Lozenges: 5–10 mg every 2–3 hours during the first 48 hours. Recommended: Cold-Eeze Lozenges.
  • Vitamin C: 1,000 mg every 4–6 hours for 2–3 days.
  • Quercetin: 500 mg twice daily. Helps reduce inflammation. Recommended: NOW Foods Quercetin.
  • NAC (N-Acetylcysteine): 600 mg twice daily for respiratory health. Recommended: Thorne NAC.
  • Elderberry Extract: 1–2 teaspoons twice daily. Antiviral support. Recommended: Gaia Herbs Elderberry Syrup.

Additional Tips

  • Drink plenty of fluids with electrolytes. Recommended: Liquid I.V. Hydration Multiplier.
  • Gargle with warm salt water for throat relief (1/2 tsp salt in warm water).
  • Inhale steam for 5–10 minutes to ease congestion.
  • Prioritize rest to allow your immune system to recover.
